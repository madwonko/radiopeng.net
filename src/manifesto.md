## The RadioPeng Manifesto

RadioPeng exists because music deserves better than optimization.

We believe in **listening**, not skipping.  
In letting a track unfold, even if it’s awkward, quiet, long, or unfamiliar.  
In trusting that curiosity is a better guide than metrics.

RadioPeng is not a playlist.  
It is not an algorithm.  
It does not know what you “might like.”

It is a signal sent by people.

---

### We believe:

- That freeform radio is a cultural act.
- That context matters.
- That DJs are not content providers but *participants*.
- That a bad transition can be more honest than a perfect one.
- That silence is sometimes the most radical programming choice.

We believe music can be:
beautiful, abrasive, patient, wrong, unresolved, funny, devotional, stubborn.

Sometimes all at once.

---

### What we broadcast:

Records found late at night.  
Music that didn’t test well.  
Things that don’t belong together but insist on being played back to back.  
Sounds with fingerprints still on them.

We play music because someone thought it mattered — not because it trended.

---

### What we refuse:

- Engagement farming.
- Endless sameness disguised as personalization.
- The idea that culture must be efficient to survive.
- The belief that everything must be explained immediately.

RadioPeng does not hurry.

---

### How we operate:

RadioPeng is independent and listener-supported.  
It survives on trust, generosity, and shared attention.

There are no corporate playlists here.  
No sponsored moods.  
No “you might also enjoy.”

Just transmission.

---

### To the listener:

You don’t have to like everything we play.  
You don’t have to understand it.  
You just have to stay long enough to let it happen.

If something here makes you pause, lean in, or wonder why it was chosen —  
then the signal worked.
