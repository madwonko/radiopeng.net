<p data-start="292" data-end="662">In a music world obsessed with speed and spectacle, <strong data-start="344" data-end="375">Natural Information Society</strong> (NIS) invites us to slow down, listen deeply, and get lost in repetition. Led by multi-instrumentalist and composer <strong data-start="492" data-end="509">Joshua Abrams</strong>, this Chicago-based collective creates mesmerizing soundscapes that feel less like songs and more like sonic environments — rich, meditative, and alive.</p>
<p data-start="664" data-end="981">At the heart of NIS’s sound is the <strong data-start="699" data-end="710">guimbri</strong>, a three-stringed bass lute traditionally used in Gnawa music of North Africa. Abrams plays it not as an exotic accent, but as a <em data-start="840" data-end="860">foundational pulse</em>, anchoring long-form compositions that blur the lines between jazz, minimalism, African rhythms, and experimental drone.</p>

<h3 data-start="983" data-end="1016">🎵 What Does It <em data-start="1003" data-end="1010">Sound</em> Like?</h3>
<p data-start="1018" data-end="1026">Imagine:</p>

<ul data-start="1027" data-end="1233">
 	<li data-start="1027" data-end="1080">
<p data-start="1029" data-end="1080">The hypnotic grooves of <strong data-start="1053" data-end="1060">Can</strong> or <strong data-start="1064" data-end="1079">Terry Riley</strong>.</p>
</li>
 	<li data-start="1081" data-end="1152">
<p data-start="1083" data-end="1152">The spiritual resonance of <strong data-start="1110" data-end="1129">Pharoah Sanders</strong> or <a href="https://radiopeng.org/alice-coltrane-and-the-harp-of-cosmic-funk/"><strong data-start="1133" data-end="1151">Alice Coltrane</strong></a>.</p>
</li>
 	<li data-start="1153" data-end="1233">
<p data-start="1155" data-end="1233">The patience and spatial awareness of <a href="https://radiopeng.org/brian-eno-the-man-who-made-silence-weird/"><strong data-start="1193" data-end="1206">Brian Eno</strong></a>, but with organic texture.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-start="1235" data-end="1433">NIS tracks often unfold slowly, building subtle changes over extended durations. It's not music for the impatient, but for the curious. For those who want to <em data-start="1393" data-end="1411">sit inside sound</em>, not just consume it.</p>

<h3 data-start="1435" data-end="1466">🌀 Music as a Living System</h3>
<p data-start="1468" data-end="1730">Abrams and his rotating group of collaborators treat music like an ecology — everything interacts, but nothing dominates. The ensemble often includes harmonium, bass clarinet, cornet, electric guitar, and percussion, all woven into intricate, looping structures.</p>
<p data-start="1732" data-end="1961">There’s no traditional soloing. Instead, the musicians contribute to a shared momentum, like murmuring birds in a flock or ripples in water. It’s a collective act of presence and listening — both for the players and the audience.</p>

<p data-start="1988" data-end="2045">If you’re new to Natural Information Society, start with:</p>

 	<li data-start="2047" data-end="2136">

 	<li data-start="2233" data-end="2378">
<p data-start="2235" data-end="2378"><strong data-start="2235" data-end="2285">“Descension (Out of Our Constrictions)” (2021)</strong> – A joyous, ecstatic collaboration with <strong data-start="2326" data-end="2343">Bitchin Bajas</strong>, built around movement and trance.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3 data-start="2380" data-end="2418">🔊 A Live Experience Like No Other</h3>
<p data-start="2420" data-end="2675">Live, NIS transforms venues into sanctuaries of sound. Performances often stretch into hour-long sets without pause, inviting audiences into a meditative headspace. It’s less about clapping or crowd response and more about <em data-start="2643" data-end="2674">experiencing time differently</em>.</p>

<h3 data-start="2677" data-end="2698">🌍 Why It Matters</h3>
<p data-start="2700" data-end="2925">Natural Information Society is doing something quietly radical: reminding us that music can be sacred without being religious, experimental without being alienating, and deeply grounded in tradition without being bound to it.</p>
<p data-start="2927" data-end="3040">In an age of noise, their music is a form of care — an invitation to slow down, tune in, and move with intention.</p>
